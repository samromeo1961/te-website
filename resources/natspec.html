üìö About NATSPEC

üá¶üá∫ What is NATSPEC?

NATSPEC is the National Building Specification system for Australia. It provides standardised worksection templates for construction specifications, aligned with the National Construction Code (NCC) and Australian Standards.

NATSPEC is developed and maintained by Construction Information Systems Limited, a not-for-profit organisation owned by government and industry bodies. It has been serving the Australian construction industry since 1975.

Worksections are provided as Microsoft Word templates that specifiers edit to suit each project's requirements. NATSPEC is updated twice yearly (April and October) to align with NCC updates and changes to Australian Standards.

üìã NATSPEC vs AUS-SPEC

NATSPEC is for building construction ‚Äî used by architects, interior designers, landscape architects, structural engineers, and building services engineers.

AUS-SPEC is the Local Government specification system for life-cycle management of public assets including roads, bridges, utilities, and urban infrastructure. It is maintained by NATSPEC since 2007 in partnership with IPWEA.

This reference tool includes both NATSPEC building worksections (Groups 01-10) and AUS-SPEC infrastructure worksections (Groups 00, 11-18).

üî¢ Understanding Worksection Numbers

NATSPEC uses the National Classification System to organise worksections hierarchically:

Groups (2 digits): 00-18 represent major categories such as Structure (03), Enclosure (04), Interior (05), etc.

Sections (3 digits): Subdivisions within groups, e.g., 031 = Concrete, 041 = Roof cladding

Worksections (4 digits): Specific specifications, e.g., 0311 = Concrete formwork, 0314 = Concrete in situ
